Important Definitions:

The chemical/physical/biological compliance status in the graph is categorized into three groups:

  • Partial Compliant Chemical/Physical/Biological:

    Indicates that only a few parameters are within the permissible limit, while the remaining parameters are not measured.

  • Not Compliant Chemical/Physical/Biological:

    Samples fail to comply with the JJM guidelines. Even if a single parameter exceeds the permissible limit, the sample is considered not compliant.

  • JJM Compliant Chemical/Physical/Biological:

    Represents samples where all parameters have been measured, and their readings fall within the permissible limit. It signifies full compliance with the JJM requirements.

Physical Compliance

Physical compliance ensures that water meets certain visual and sensory criteria, such as clarity, color, and odor.

Chemical Compliance

Chemical compliance focuses on the presence and concentration of various chemical contaminants in water.

Bacteriological Compliance

Bacteriological compliance is crucial for assessing the microbiological quality of water.
