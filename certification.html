Analysis of Water Quality, Quantity, Coverage, and Certification

Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) Village Status Classification:

The JJM has classified villages into three categories based on their level of implementation and access to Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC).

Certified:

Villages that have 100% FHTC coverage and all tested Parameters are under the permissible limits standarized by Jal Jeevan Mission.

Reported:

Villages that claims to have 100% FHTC coverage and all parameters under permissible limits

Ongoing:

Villages have not reached a significant level of FHTC coverage, they are in-progress

Observation JJM Status:

The observation of JJM status considers three significant factors:

Water Quality:

Provide safe and pure water that is tested to be under the permissible limits of Physical, Chemical and Bacteriological Parameters


Water Quality BIS 10500 Standard should be satisfied

See Standards (at page 21)

Water Quantity:

Supply of drinking water in a household, the supply must be 55+ litres per day for certification

Coverage:

The measure of Extent of FHTC coverage. It must be 100% for certification

To be declared as "Certified" under observation of JJM status, a village must fulfill all three factors.
